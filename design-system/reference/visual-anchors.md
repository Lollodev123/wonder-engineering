# Anonymized visual anchors

The source reference media is not part of this public repository. These notes retain only the reusable observations used by the prototype.

## Idle wall

- A dark matte gallery surrounds a 5×3 grid of luminous monitors.
- A person silhouette at one side makes the installation's scale legible.
- A single visual can be divided across neighbouring tiles.
- Surrounding tiles use restrained gradient variation.
- Small vertical offsets make the coordinated wave visible.

## Focused detail

- The room recedes and the composition tightens around fewer tiles.
- One tile carries a large number or image.
- Two or three short callout pills support the focused content.
- Thin connection lines are optional and used sparingly.

## Interaction state

- Most of the wall dims while the actionable tile is highlighted.
- A hand or person establishes that the change was triggered, not ambient.
- The selected state must read without explanatory copy.

## Detached content cluster

- One primary portrait or image anchors the cluster.
- Smaller images and labels orbit it without becoming a dashboard.
- Soft gradients and negative space preserve hierarchy.

## Quality checks derived from the references

- The wall should feel physically located, not like a webpage grid.
- Motion and screen content should change in the same narrative beat.
- A still frame is insufficient: timing, transition paths, and the loop seam must be inspected in motion.
- No more than three supporting labels should compete with the focused tile.
